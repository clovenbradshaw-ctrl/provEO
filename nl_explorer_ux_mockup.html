The Private Police and the Public Budget

The Nashville Downtown Partnership received $4.2 million in public funds during fiscal year 2025, a 14.3 percent increase over the prior year, according to budget documents obtained from Metro Finance. The increase followed a motion by Councilmember Torres on March 7 to amend the public safety allocation.

The Partnership has contracted with Solaren International for private security services since 2019, at a cost the organization describes as $280,000 annually. Internal documents reviewed by this reporter put the figure at $312,000, a discrepancy the Partnership has not publicly explained.

Metro's Office of Homeless Services did not respond to requests for comment about the overlap between the Partnership's security footprint and OHS encampment clearance operations. No disclosure of the Solaren contract appears in the Partnership's public filings for the period.
